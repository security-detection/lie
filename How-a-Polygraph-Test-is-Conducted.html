How a Polygraph Test is Conducted

Actually, there really is no such thing. What is actually being referred to is a polygraph test, which works as a ‘truth detector’. So let’s talk about a polygraph. A polygraph is a machine that collects data from a number of physiological sensors lightly attached to a person, and records them. The test is comprised of questions created to elicit a response from the examinee. These responses are then related to reactions that have been proven through research to be highly associated with deception. The absence of indications deception, irregular or missing data is indicative of one telling the truth.A lot of preparation is required to properly conduct a polygraph (https://www.apapolygraph.org/accredited-programs). This can last for up to 2 hours, with the specific issue questions being a small part of it all.Examinees must be made familiar with the procedures, polygraph equipment (https://limestonetech.com/products/preftest-pro-suite/) and types of questions. Many preliminary questions, baseline questions, establishing questions...both known true and untrue, are asked and responses recorded. By time the test it taken, it is quite familiar and basic. Nerves do not enter into it. All such considerations are taken into account. All questions on the specific issue are gone over more than once, with no chance taken for incorrect meanings or misunderstandings. All in all it is a pretty straight forward and simple, if somewhat boring, procedure.
